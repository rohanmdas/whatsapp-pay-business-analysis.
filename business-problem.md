# Business Problem

WhatsApp has over 2 billion users worldwide and is one of the most widely used messaging platforms. Despite its strong user base, the platform historically focused on communication rather than financial transactions.

India represents one of the largest digital payments markets due to the rapid adoption of the Unified Payments Interface (UPI). Competitors such as Google Pay, PhonePe, and Paytm dominate this ecosystem by enabling seamless peer-to-peer money transfers and merchant payments.

The introduction of WhatsApp Pay presents an opportunity to leverage the existing WhatsApp network to enable integrated digital payments directly within the messaging platform.

However, launching a payment platform requires careful analysis of regulatory compliance, system scalability, user adoption, and secure transaction infrastructure.

This project analyzes the requirements, stakeholders, and process workflows necessary to successfully implement WhatsApp Pay within the Indian digital payments ecosystem.

## Key Challenges

- Regulatory compliance with NPCI and Reserve Bank of India guidelines
- Ensuring secure and reliable payment transactions
- Competing with established UPI payment platforms
- Achieving rapid user adoption within an already competitive market

## Project Objective

The objective of this analysis is to evaluate how WhatsApp Pay can be successfully implemented by identifying key stakeholders, designing payment workflows, and defining functional and non-functional requirements for a scalable payment system.
